Classic Meatloaf


Description

Originating from American cuisine, the traditional meatloaf is a classic and comforting dish which has been a stable of the homely kitchen for generations. Meatloaf is prepared from minced meat, eggs, bread crumbs and a mix of spices.

Ingredients

  • Ground chuck
  • Vegetables (carrot, celery, onion, bellpepper, mushrooms, garlic)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire Sauce
  • 1 egg
  • Spices (dried Italian herbs, salt, pepper, and cayenne pepper)
  • Bread Crumbs
  • Glaze (made out of brown sugar, ketchup, Dijon mustard, and sriracha)

Steps

  1. Chop the vegetables and combine them and the garlic in a food processor until nearly pureed.
  2. Form the meatloaf by combining the vegetables, ground chuck, Worcestershire sauce, spices, and egg. Sprinkle in the bread crumbs and mix them with your hands (be careful not to overmix).
  3. Form the meat mixture into a loaf and place it in a prepared baking pan. Bake for about 15 minutes.
  4. After the 15 minutes, combine brown sugar, ketchup, Dijon, and sriracha to make the glaze. Spoon the glaze over the slightly hot meatloaf.
  5. Return meatloaf to the oven and bake until the internal temperature reaches 160 degrees F (usually takes 60-75 minutes).
